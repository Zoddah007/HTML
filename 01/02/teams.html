The NBA is comprised of 30 teams, each with a roster of talented players from around the globe. These teams are split into two conferences, the Eastern and Western, and further divided into six divisions. Each team's roster can have up to 15 players during the regular season, plus three more on two-way contracts. Some of the most recognizable players in the league include: * **LeBron James**, a forward on the Los Angeles Lakers, considered one of the greatest players of all time. * **Stephen Curry**, a guard for the Golden State Warriors, known for his revolutionary three-point shooting. * **Nikola Jokic**, the star center for the Denver Nuggets and a multiple-time MVP award winner. * **Giannis Antetokounmpo**, a forward for the Milwaukee Bucks and another dominant force in the league. * **Luka Dončić**, the star guard for the Dallas Mavericks, who has become one of the most exciting young players in the NBA. The dynamic nature of the league means that team rosters are constantly shifting due to trades, free agency, and the annual NBA Draft, which introduces new talent. This constant movement of players and the formation of new alliances is a major factor in the league's competitive and ever-changing landscape.
